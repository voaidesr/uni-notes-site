# Paradoxul St. Petersburg 

$$
X \sim 
\begin{pmatrix}
2^0 & 2^1 & 2^2  & \dots & 2^n & \dots \\
p & qp & q^2 p & \dots & q^np & \dots
\end{pmatrix}
$$
Media 
$$
\mathbb{E}[X] = \infty
$$
Avem $p = \frac{1}{6} \quad q = \frac{5}{6}$

Bernoulli expected value
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{BEV}[X] &= \prod_{n \geq 1} 2^{n q^n p} =  2^{
\sum_{n\geq 1} p n q^n
} \\
\\&= 2 ^ {
\sum_{n \geq 1} pq \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}q}(q^n)
} \\
\\&= 2^{
pq \frac{\mathrm{d}}{\mathrm{d}q} ( \frac{1}{1-q}) = 
}\\
\\&= 2^{pq \cdot (1-q)^{-2}} \\
\\&= 2^{\frac{q}{p}} = 2^5
\end{aligned}
$$
Dacă avem $p = q =\frac{1}{2}$, atunci:
$$
\mathbb{E}[X] = \infty \qquad \mathrm{BEV}[X] = 2
$$
